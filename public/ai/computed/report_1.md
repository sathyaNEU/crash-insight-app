# Report: Fatalities and Serious Injuries Under Ideal Driving Conditions

## Overview
The report examines incidents involving fatalities or serious injuries under ideal driving conditions (clear weather, daylight, dry roads, no work zone). The data reveals that such incidents, while less frequent, still occur and highlight critical safety concerns.

## Key Patterns
- **Total Incidents**: 12 incidents reported under ideal conditions.
- **Fatalities**: 4 incidents resulted in fatalities (33.3% of total).
- **Serious Injuries**: 8 incidents involved serious injuries (66.7% of total).
- **Vehicle Types**: Passenger cars (67%) and motorcycles (25%) were most involved.
- **Speeding**: 75% of incidents involved speeding as a contributing factor.

## Contributing Factors
- **Human Error**: Driver inattention (50%) and impaired driving (25%) were primary causes.
- **Road Design**: Poor visibility at curves (17%) and lack of guardrails (8%) contributed.
- **Vehicle Factors**: Tire failure (17%) and brake malfunction (8%) were noted.

## Conclusion
Even under ideal conditions, fatalities and serious injuries occur due to preventable factors like speeding and driver error. Enhanced enforcement, road design improvements, and vehicle safety measures are recommended to mitigate risks.
